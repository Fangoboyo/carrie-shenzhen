\#CAD README





\##General Notes



The entire CAD can be found in the FINAL files.



The models are split into parts, and are meant to be printed the way they are separated (one side of carrot, other half, one half of pot, other half, and individual parts that are inserted).


Pictures of complete models and renders of the carrot and the pot can be found in the main README file.



Individual parts are found in the Individual Parts Folder. 



Some individual parts don't contain their own file, and are in other files (such as some of the mounting/holding mechanisms, which exist in the carrot file).



There are some parts that are just created within the FINAL file, and do not exist in any other file.





\##Part Mounting/Connecting Parts


During testing, we found that due to the size of our project and the size of the printer nozzle, all of the joints that we created were extremely weak. This made us resort to testing just using the friction between the parts, which while working, are not reliable.



To fortify this approach, when we build our project, we will utilize 3D modeling glue (PLA glue), to directly create a connection in addition to cylindrical connectors. 



\##Parts



Some of the parts in the build are not apart of what is 3D printed. In the Carrot, you will see 3 rectangular boxes, with the middle one with some stuff sticking out. Those 3 are just placeholders for where our RV1103 Pico Mini Linux SBC, Carry PCB, and Battery Regulator will be. 



The battery will be placed in the battery holder, and will be stopped on the top by the part on the other side of the carrot, so there is no vertical movement.



The camera is held at the top of the carrot (toward the leaf), with cylinders that fit inside it's mounting holes, and is slotted in there.



The PCB and microcontroller for the Pot will be slotted at the bottom of the Pot.

The PCB and microcontroller for both the Pot and the Carrot will be connected to each other via pin headers. These pin headers will be cut to be shorter than standard size, in order to fit inside compartments. 



Components will be connected to each other via wiring and soldering. 

The Cap and the Leaf are attached using a the coil method (like a bottle cap).
